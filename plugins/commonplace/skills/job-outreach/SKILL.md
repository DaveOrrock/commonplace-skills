# Outreach & referrals

**REQUIRED BACKGROUND:** job-hunt-hq, job-evidence-mining.

The channel math is brutal and underused: referred candidates convert to interview at ~40% vs low single digits for cold inbound, senior hires skew heavily to sourced/referral, and most searches still spend ~85% of effort on cold applications. Every A-tier role should get a **channel check before the cold apply**: who do we already know?

## The candidate dossier (build once, reuse everywhere)

Headhunters pitch candidates in third person; candidates never write that artifact for themselves. Maintain one as its own note, `{{dossier}}`: a half-page, third-person submission brief — positioning line, three locker-backed proof points, comp band, availability, links. Two uses: (a) forwardable copy for anyone offering a referral, so champions pitch accurately instead of vaguely; (b) the source paragraph for every outreach message, keeping the story consistent.

## Dormant-connection sweep (the vault knows people you forgot)

Before any cold application to an A/B-tier company: search `{{contacts}}` and the whole vault (semantic + `find_unlinked_mentions`) for people connected to the target — ex-colleagues now there, people met at conferences, second-degree mentions in old meeting notes. A person named in an 18-month-old note who now works at the target is warmer than any cold message. Log every contact touched as a note in `{{contacts}}` with relationship, last touch, and `- introduced [[person]]` relations — introductions generated per conversation is the network's reproduction rate, and dormant high-value contacts are the cheapest channel there is.

## Messages that get replies

To recruiters/hiring managers (LinkedIn/email), the structure that works:
1. **One specific hook** about their team/product/posting (same discipline as cover-letter hooks — must not be sendable to anyone else)
2. **One line of proof** you fit (a locker number, not adjectives)
3. **One small ask**: 15 minutes, or "worth me applying?" — never "any opportunities?" + CV dump

Under ~400 characters outperforms long messages; personalized connection requests roughly double acceptance. Referral asks to acquaintances: make it effortless — the dossier attached, the specific role linked, explicit "no worries if not". Most employees are *paid* to refer plausible candidates; the ask is less of an imposition than it feels.

## Cadence

2-3 follow-ups per thread, spaced 4-7 days, then stop — politely closing the loop on the last one. Application follow-up: once, 7-10 days after applying, to a named human where one exists. Log every touch as an observation on the contact/role note (`- [touch] DM'd re Head of Eng, 2026-06-10`); the review skill's stale-deal sweep drafts the next one when it falls due. **Sent volume note:** outreach quality degrades fast with volume — 5 sharp messages beat 25 templated ones, and templated outreach burns contacts permanently.

## When to shift weight here

job-hunt-review showing healthy materials (tailored CVs, decent screen rates when seen) but low response rates = a channel problem, not a content problem. Shift hours from applications to outreach; at senior level, cold-apply-only strategies underperform structurally.

## Don'ts

- Never mass-message with a template; each message passes the "only this person" test.
- Never ask a stranger for a job — ask for 15 minutes or an opinion; jobs follow.
- Never let a referrer pitch blind — they get the dossier.
- Never follow up more than 3 times; persistence past that converts to reputation damage.
