# Offer negotiation

**REQUIRED BACKGROUND:** job-hunt-hq, job-evidence-mining (`{{comp-file}}`).

Negotiation is won by evidence collected before the offer exists. The base rates: countering lifts offers ~12-19% on average, ~94% of negotiated offers stand, and most candidates still don't counter. **Default = always counter, calmly, once, with evidence.**

## The leverage memo (compile before any number is spoken)

From accumulated vault data, write into the role note:
1. **Their eagerness** — quoted, dated `[signal]` observations from interview debriefs: shrinking reply latency, execs added late, "when could you start", accelerated rounds. This is how much they want the user, in their own behavior.
2. **Market position** — `{{comp-file}}` distribution for this role family/stage; where their band sits in it.
3. **BATNA state** — other live processes and their projected offer dates (below).
4. **Their promises** — every `[promise]` observation logged during evaluation/interviews ("2 days office after probation", "hands-on drops as you hire"). **The counter converts promises into contract terms** — this is the most natural, least adversarial ask there is: "you mentioned X; let's reflect it in the offer."

## Salary-expectations questions (pre-offer)

Deflect early naming when leverage is low ("I'd expect the band for the role — what is it?"); pay-transparency norms mean they usually have one. If forced to name: anchor at the top of the comp-file's justifiable range, never below the user's `{{profile}}` floor. A floor criterion exists so that desperation can't renegotiate it mid-call.

## Offer-window synchronization (the BATNA is engineered, not lucky)

Throughout the search, model each live process's expected offer date from its observed stage velocity (dates are in the role notes). Recommend pacing moves so offers land within the same ~10 days: ask a slower process to accelerate ("I'm in late stages elsewhere" — true, verifiable, and itself an eagerness test), start a fast process later, or ask an early offer for a decision-date extension. A competing offer is the single strongest lever; near-misses in timing are the usual reason candidates don't have one.

## The counter

One counter, specific, evidence-framed: number (or top-2 priorities: base + one other), promises-to-terms list, enthusiasm explicit ("I want to sign this week"). Get the final offer **in writing** before accepting. Equity: ask for strike price, latest 409a/valuation, preference stack, vesting cliff — "generously benchmarked" is not a number.

**Folklore check:** the "80% of counter-offer acceptors leave within a year" stat (for staying at a current employer) has no traceable basis — don't repeat it; the *directional* caution (underlying reasons persist) is fair.

## Acceptance diligence

Before signing: company memo's kill criteria re-checked and current; references choreographed — referees briefed on the live offer, talking points drafted, and their story consistent with what interviews heard (back-channels happen); start date leaves real rest; resignation plan written.

## Don'ts

- Never accept on the call — gratitude now, decision after the written offer and a re-run of job-evaluation against the *original* criteria.
- Never bluff a competing offer; synchronization exists so it's true.
- Never negotiate in dribs (number today, holiday tomorrow) — one consolidated counter.
- Never let an exploding deadline force skipping diligence; a real offer survives "I need until Friday".
