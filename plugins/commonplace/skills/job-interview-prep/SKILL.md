# Interview prep

**REQUIRED BACKGROUND:** job-hunt-hq, job-evidence-mining (stories), job-company-research (interviewer film study).

Interviews are prepared *from the vault*, debriefed *into the vault*. Every loop makes the next one easier — questions, answers that landed, eagerness signals, and weaknesses all accumulate.

## Before a round: the prep brief

Build one note per round in `{{debriefs}}` (prep on top, debrief appended after):

1. **Predicted questions** — from the JD's top requirements, the round type, this company's reported interview style (research note), and the **question bank**: every question ever logged across the search, weighted by what's recurred 3+ times. The heatmap rule: any recurring question whose past answers the user rated below "good" is automatically prep item #1.
2. **Story selection** — map predicted questions to evidence-locker `[story]`/`[win]` entries in STAR shape (Situation, Task, Action, Result — result carries the exact locker number). Aim for 6-8 stories covering: delivery under pressure, conflict, scaling/org-building, failure + what changed, influencing without authority, technical judgment. Check the **consistency rule**: stories and "why I'm leaving" phrasing must match what was said in earlier rounds (grep prior debriefs) and what referees will say.
3. **Film study** — interviewer notes from `{{contacts}}`: their background, likely pet questions, who's the skeptic, what each ego-invests in.
4. **Collection plan** — interviews are diligence in both directions. 2-3 specific questions whose answers feed the company memo's open items and kill criteria ("how do priorities get set week to week?" tests the Glassdoor theme; "what happened to the last person in this role?"). Asking sharp diligence questions also reads as senior.
5. **Pregame file** — 10 minutes of the user's own best evidence: strongest locker wins, best-rated past answers, a reminder of leverage status. It's a record, not affirmations — confidence from documentation.

## After a round: the debrief (same day, 15 minutes)

Append to the round note, as observations so they aggregate:
- `- [question] <verbatim as close as possible> #<topic>` — feeds the bank
- `- [self-rating] <question> — answer felt weak/good/strong (why)` — feeds the heatmap
- `- [signal] <eagerness/coolness>: reply latency, "when could you start?", execs added to the loop, next-step speed` — **feeds the leverage journal job-negotiation compiles**
- `- [intel] <collection-plan answers>` — feeds the company memo; update kill criteria if touched
- Anything promised verbally → `- [promise] …` on the role note (job-evaluation discounts it; job-negotiation converts it)

Update role `status`. If the round went badly: log it factually; the autopsy discipline lives in job-hunt-review (48h quarantine — no same-day spiraling analysis).

## When interviews aren't converting

3+ loops without advancing → stop generic practice. Run the heatmap over the question bank and self-ratings; the recurring-weak-answer list *is* the diagnosis. Deeper pattern analysis (competing hypotheses) belongs to job-hunt-review.

## Don'ts

- No invented stories, no inflated numbers — every claim must survive "tell me more about that".
- Don't skip the debrief because the round felt fine; signals and questions decay overnight.
- Don't rehearse scripts verbatim — prep bullets, not paragraphs; recruiters flag recited answers.
