# Cover letters

**REQUIRED BACKGROUND:** job-hunt-hq, job-evidence-mining.

## Why letters matter again

Most hiring managers do read them — many *before* the CV — and a strong letter can get a borderline candidate an interview. More importantly: AI flooded applications with identical polished-generic letters, so **specificity is now the signal that polish used to be**. A letter that could be sent to any company is worse than no letter; a letter with verifiable, company-specific content stands out precisely because the slop can't fake it.

Write one when: the application accepts it and the role is A/B-tier; there's connective tissue the CV can't carry (career pivot, gap, relocation, "why this company"); the company is small or mission-driven. Skip when: C-tier volume applications with optional letters — spend the time on a better-targeted A-tier application instead.

## Structure (250-400 words, three moves)

1. **The hook (most-read paragraph — earn it):** one specific, true sentence connecting you to *this* company — their product, a real thing they shipped, the problem their market has — plus your positioning in one line. Test: could this sentence appear in a letter to any other company? Rewrite until no.
2. **The proof:** one or two evidence-locker stories matched to the JD's top requirement. Numbers, context, outcome — *new information or new framing*, never a restatement of CV bullets.
3. **The close:** why this role is the logical next step for you (the "connective tissue"), one line; no begging, no "I'd welcome the opportunity to discuss".

## The slop filter (run before sending)

Discard-on-sight tells recruiters report — rewrite if any appear:
- Generic enthusiasm: "I'm excited to leverage my passion for innovation", "I was thrilled to see this opportunity"
- Uniform sentence rhythm, perfect-but-empty prose, buzzword chains ("results-driven leader with a proven track record")
- Mirroring the job posting's own phrasing back at it
- Em-dash-dense prose (unfairly, a flagged "AI tell" — write around it)
- Zero facts that required knowing anything about the company

**The test that matters: every paragraph contains something only this candidate could write to only this company.**

## Process

Read role note + company note (research observations supply the hook), draft, run the slop filter, honesty pass (claims locker-backed), save to `{{applications}}` and register on the role note alongside the CV version (`- submitted [[…]]`). Match the user's voice — letters read aloud should sound like them, not like a model.

Application-form free-text answers ("why us?") are mini-letters: same hook discipline, 50-150 words, same slop filter. These get screened hardest because identical AI answers cluster.

## Don'ts

- Never one template lightly edited across companies — recruiters see the application *set*; clusters get binned.
- Never restate the CV — the letter exists for what the CV can't say.
- Never exceed one page / ~400 words; intro carries the weight.
- Never send without the slop filter and an out-loud read.
